FCSys.Characteristics.'H+'.Gas

H+ gas

Information

Assumptions:

  1. The radius (for the purpose of the rigid-sphere assumption of kinetic theory) is that of H (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen).

Additional notes:

  • The source data [McBride2002] breaks the data into three temperature ranges, but the constants are the same for each. Therefore, the temperature limits are set here such that the entire range is handled without switching. The lower temperature limit in the source data is 298.150 K, but here it is expanded down to 200 K. The constants are independent of temperature anyway.
  • The enthalpy to produce H+ from H2O (H2O ⇀ OH- + H+) is -96569.804 J/mol. The enthalpy to produce H+ from H3O+ (H3O+ ⇀ H2O + H+) is 839826.0 J/mol. Based on Tissandier et al., the enthalpy of formation of aqueous H+ is 1150.1e3 J/mol [Tissandier1998].

For more information, see the Characteristic record.

Extends from BaseClasses.Characteristic (Record for thermodynamic and resistive properties).

Modelica definition

record Gas "H+ gas"
  import Data = Modelica.Media.IdealGases.Common.SingleGasesData.Hplus;

  extends BaseClasses.Characteristic(
    final formula="H+",
    phase="gas",
    final m=Data.MM*U.kg/U.mol,
    b_v=[1],
    specVolPow={-1,0},
    Deltah0_f=Data.MM*Data.Hf*U.J/U.mol,
    Deltah0=Data.MM*Data.H0*U.J/U.mol,
    specHeatCapPow=-2,
    T_lim_c={200.000,20000.000}*U.K,
    b_c={Data.alow} .* fill({U.K^(3 - i) for i in 1:size(Data.alow, 1)}, size(
        T_lim_c, 1) - 1),
    B_c={Data.blow} .* fill({U.K,1}, size(T_lim_c, 1) - 1) - b_c[:, 2:3]*ln(U.K),

    r=120*U.pico*U.m/U.q);

end Gas;

FCSys.Characteristics.'H+'.Solid

H+ in solid

Information

The enthalpy of formation (Deltah0_f) and the additional enthalpy offset (h_offset) are specified such that the enthalpy in the PartialSpecies model is referenced to zero at 0 °C. Otherwise, there is a very large bias that causes problems with the reaction. The proper values are not known from the NASA CEA thermodynamic data [McBride2002] (it only provides data for H+ gas). Since the same H+ model is used in the anode and cathode, there is no net effect.

The specific volume of protons corresponds to the concentration measured by Spry and Fayer (0.95 M) in Nafion® at λ = 12, where λ is the number of H2O molecules to SO3H endgroups. At λ = 22, the concentration was measured at 0.54 M [Spry2009].

Extends from Gas (H+ gas).

Modelica definition

record Solid "H+ in solid"
  extends Gas(
    final phase="solid",
    specVolPow={0,0},
    b_v=[1/(0.95*U.M)],
    p_min=-Modelica.Constants.inf,
    Deltah0_f=H2O.Gas.Deltah0_f/4,
    h_offset=-'H+'.Gas.Deltah0_f + H2O.Gas.Deltah0_f/4);

end Solid;
