Notes:
     
    
  
  • The radius of H2O is 282 pm
   (http://www.lsbu.ac.uk/water/molecule.html).  Using the radius of H
   from http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen and the center-to-center
   distance of hydrogen atoms in H2O from [Avogadro1.03],
   156.6 pm, the radius of H2O would be (120 + 156.6/2) pm = 198.3 pm.
    • 
  
  • The virial coefficients are from [Dymond2002, p. 4]).  The
  temperature range of the coefficients is [350, 770] K, but this is not enforced in the functions.
