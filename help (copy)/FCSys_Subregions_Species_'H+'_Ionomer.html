Information

Assumptions:
    
    
  1. The density of H+ is equal to that of
  C19HF37O5S- or approximately 1.912 M.  Note that
  this is greater than that measured by Spry and Fayer (see
  Characteristics.'H+'.Ionomer), but it
  simplifies the model by requiring only C19HF37O5S-
  (not C19HF37O5S) for charge neutrality.
    
    2.

model Calibrated "Correlations with adjustment factors"
  extends Species(
    redeclare replaceable package Data = Characteristics.'H+'.Ionomer (
          specVolPow=FCSys.Characteristics.'C19HF37O5S-'.Ionomer.specVolPow,
          b_v=FCSys.Characteristics.'C19HF37O5S-'.Ionomer.b_v),
    Xi=k_Xi*Data.Xi(T),
    F=k_F*Data.F(T),
    R=k_R*Data.R(T));

  parameter Q.NumberAbsolute k_Xi(final nominal=1) = 1 
    "
Adjustment factor for dynamic compressibility (kΞ)

    "
Adjustment factor for dynamic fluidity (kF)
";

    "
Adjustment factor for thermal resistivity (kα Ṡ)
";

end Calibrated;

FCSys.Subregions.Species.'H+'.Ionomer.Fixed

Fixed properties



Information


Assumptions:
    
    
  1. The generalized resistivities (Ξ, F, R) are fixed (e.g., independent of temperature).
    2. 
    
  2. The density of H+ is equal to that of
  C19HF37O5S- or approximately 1.912 M.  Note that
  this is greater than that measured by Spry and Fayer (see
  Characteristics.'H+'.Ionomer), but it
  simplifies the model by requiring only C19HF37O5S-
  (not C19HF37O5S) for charge neutrality.
    
    3. 
    


  
The default resistivities (F=1/(5.3e-6*U.Pa*U.s) and R=U.m*U.K/(0.1661*U.W)) are of H gas
  (rather than H+) at 300 K from [Schetz1996, p. 139].
