FCSys.Characteristics.C.Graphite

C graphite

Information

Assumptions:

  1. Constant specific volume (i.e., incompressible and without thermal expansion)

Additional notes:

  • The radius is from http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon. See also http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_der_Waals_radius.
  • The default specific volume (v=U.cm^3*m/(2.210*U.g)) is of pyrolytic graphite at 300 K according to Incropera and DeWitt [Incropera2002, p. 909]. Other forms are (also at 300 K and based on the same reference) are:
    • Amorphous carbon: v=U.cm^3*m/(1.950*U.g)
    • Diamond (type IIa): v=U.cm^3*m/(3.500*U.g)

    For more information, see the Characteristic record.
